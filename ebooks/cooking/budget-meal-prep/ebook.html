1. Introduction

If you're tired of spending $15 on a mediocre lunch or wondering what's for dinner at 6:00 PM, this guide is for you. Meal prepping is for anyone who wants to reclaim their time and keep their hard-earned dollars.

By spending just 2-3 hours on a Sunday, you can ensure that you have healthy, delicious meals ready to go for the next five days.

2. Budget Shopping List

  • Inventory First: Check what you have before buying more.
  • The Staples: Stock up on grains (rice, oats, pasta), proteins (beans, eggs, frozen chicken), and hardy veggies (onions, carrots, potatoes).
  • Seasonal Produce: Buy what's on sale. Swap ingredients based on price.
  • Avoid Pre-Cut: Chop it yourself to save 50%.

3. The Sunday Prep Plan

Hour 0: Start your grains (rice, beans, pasta).

Hour 0.5: Chop and start roasting veggies and chicken.

Hour 1.0: Cook ground meats or sauces on the stovetop.

Hour 2.0: Portion everything into containers.

4. Essential Tools

  • Reliable Containers: Airtight glass or BPA-free plastic.
  • Sharp Chef’s Knife: Essential for efficient chopping.
  • Large Baking Sheets: For high-volume roasting.

5. 15 Reheat-Ready Recipes

Breakfasts

1. Veggie & Feta Egg Bites

Breakfast | Quick Reheat

Whisk 10 eggs with chopped spinach and feta. Pour into a muffin tin and bake at 350°F for 20 mins.

2. Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal

Breakfast | Batch Cook

Mix 2 cups oats, 2 cups milk, 1 egg, chopped apples, and cinnamon. Bake in a square pan at 350°F for 35 mins.

3. Banana Bread Overnight Oats

Breakfast | No-Cook

Mash a banana in a jar with 1/2 cup oats, 1/2 cup milk, and a pinch of walnuts. Refrigerate overnight.

Lunches & Dinners

4. Turkey & Sweet Potato Chili

Lunch/Dinner | Freezer Friendly

Brown ground turkey, add diced sweet potatoes, canned beans, and tomatoes. Simmer for 30 mins.

5. Sheet Pan Chicken & Root Veggies

Lunch/Dinner | Easy Prep

Toss chicken cubes, carrots, and potatoes in oil and rosemary. Roast at 400°F for 25 mins.

6. Black Bean & Corn Quinoa Bowls

Lunch/Dinner | Plant-Based

Cook quinoa. Mix with black beans, corn, salsa, and lime juice. Great cold or warm.

7. Honey Garlic Tofu Stir-Fry

Lunch/Dinner | Plant-Based

Sear cubed tofu, add frozen veggies and a soy/honey sauce. Serve over prepped rice.

8. Lentil "Bolognese" Pasta

Lunch/Dinner | Hearty

Use cooked lentils instead of meat in a standard marinara sauce. Toss with whole wheat pasta.

9. Peanut Noodle Salad

Lunch | Best Cold

Cold noodles tossed in peanut butter, soy sauce, and sriracha with shredded cabbage.

10. Beef & Broccoli Skillet

Dinner | High Protein

Sliced flank steak or ground beef sautéed with garlic, ginger, and broccoli.

11. Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

Lunch | No Reheat

Chickpeas, cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onion in a lemon-herb dressing. Keeps for 4 days.

12. Salsa Verde Chicken Casserole

Dinner | Comfort Food

Shredded chicken, brown rice, salsa verde, and a sprinkle of cheese. Bake until bubbly.

13. Curried Chickpea & Spinach Stew

Dinner | Flavorful

Canned chickpeas simmered in coconut milk and curry powder with fresh spinach added at the end.

14. Pesto Pasta with Cherry Tomatoes

Lunch | Simple

Whole grain pasta tossed in pesto with halved cherry tomatoes and white beans.

15. Turkey Burger Bowls

Dinner | Low Carb Option

Ground turkey patties (crumbled) served over lettuce with pickles, onions, and a side of roasted potato wedges.

6. Storage & Safety

  • Cool First: Let food stop steaming before closing lids to prevent sogginess.
  • The 4-Day Rule: Most prepped food is best eaten within 4 days. Freeze anything for later in the week.
  • Labeling: Always mark your containers with dates.
