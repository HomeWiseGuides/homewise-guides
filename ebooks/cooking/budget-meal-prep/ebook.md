### Introduction: The Power of Meal Prep
Welcome to "Budget Meal Prep: Cook Once, Eat All Week." If you're tired of spending $15 on a mediocre lunch or wondering what's for dinner at 6:00 PM, this guide is for you. Meal prepping isn't just for bodybuilders or professional chefs—it's for anyone who wants to reclaim their time and keep their hard-earned dollars.

By spending just 2-3 hours on a Sunday, you can ensure that you have healthy, delicious meals ready to go for the next five days. This guide will show you how to do it without breaking the bank.

---

### Mastering the Budget Shopping List
The secret to a cheap meal prep is a disciplined shopping list.
*   **Inventory First:** Check your pantry and fridge before you go. Don't buy a second jar of cumin if you already have one.
*   **The Staples:** Stock up on "anchor" ingredients that are cheap in bulk:
    *   Grains: Brown rice, oats, pasta, quinoa.
    *   Proteins: Beans (dried or canned), eggs, frozen chicken breast, ground turkey.
    *   Veggies: Onions, carrots, potatoes, frozen broccoli, spinach.
*   **Seasonal Produce:** Buy what's on sale. If bell peppers are $2 each, swap them for zucchini or carrots.
*   **Avoid Pre-Cut:** Buying whole carrots and chopping them yourself takes 2 minutes and saves 50%.

---

### The Sunday Prep Plan
A successful prep depends on a plan. Follow this workflow:
1.  **Grains & Legumes (Hour 0):** Get your rice, pasta, or beans boiling first. They take the longest but require zero attention.
2.  **Roasting (Hour 0.5):** Chop your veggies and chicken. Toss them in oil and spices and get them in the oven.
3.  **Stovetop (Hour 1.0):** While items are roasting, cook your ground meats or sauces on the stove.
4.  **Assembly (Hour 2.0):** Portion everything into containers once they've cooled slightly.

### Essential Tools for Success
You don't need fancy gadgets, but these make life easier:
*   **Reliable Containers:** Glass or BPA-free plastic containers with airtight lids.
*   **A Sharp Chef’s Knife:** Makes chopping 5 lbs of veggies a breeze rather than a chore.
*   **Large Baking Sheets:** For "sheet pan" meals.
*   **Slow Cooker or Instant Pot (Optional):** Great for set-it-and-forget-it proteins.

### 15 Recipes That Reheat Perfectly

#### Breakfasts

1. **Veggie & Feta Egg Bites**
   - *Prep:* Whisk 10 eggs with chopped spinach and feta. Pour into a muffin tin.
   - *Cook:* Bake at 350°F for 20 mins.
   - *Reheat:* 30 seconds in the microwave.

2. **Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal**
   - *Prep:* Mix 2 cups oats, 2 cups milk, 1 egg, chopped apples, and cinnamon.
   - *Cook:* Bake in a square pan at 350°F for 35 mins.
   - *Storage:* Slice into squares and refrigerate.

3. **Banana Bread Overnight Oats**
   - *Prep:* Mash a banana in a jar with 1/2 cup oats, 1/2 cup milk, and a pinch of walnuts.
   - *Storage:* Refrigerate overnight. No cooking required!

#### Lunches & Dinners

4. **Turkey & Sweet Potato Chili**
   - *Prep:* Brown ground turkey, add diced sweet potatoes, canned beans, and diced tomatoes.
   - *Cook:* Simmer for 30 mins until potatoes are soft.
   - *Why it preps well:* Flavors actually improve after a day in the fridge.

5. **Sheet Pan Chicken & Roasted Root Veggies**
   - *Prep:* Chicken breast cubes, carrots, and potatoes tossed in olive oil and rosemary.
   - *Cook:* Roast at 400°F for 25 mins.

6. **Black Bean & Corn Quinoa Bowls**
   - *Prep:* Cook quinoa. Mix with black beans, corn, salsa, and lime juice.
   - *Storage:* Great cold or warm.

7. **Honey Garlic Tofu Stir-Fry**
   - *Prep:* Press tofu, cube it, and sear. Add frozen stir-fry veggies and a simple soy/honey sauce.
   - *Storage:* Serve over rice.

8. **Lentil "Bolognese" Pasta**
   - *Prep:* Use cooked lentils instead of meat in a standard marinara sauce.
   - *Storage:* Toss with whole wheat pasta.

9. **Peanut Noodle Salad**
   - *Prep:* Cold noodles tossed in peanut butter, soy sauce, and sriracha with shredded cabbage.
   - *Note:* Do not reheat—this is best eaten cold!

10. **Beef & Broccoli Skillet**
    - *Prep:* Sliced flank steak or ground beef sautéed with garlic, ginger, and broccoli.

11. **Mediterranean Chickpea Salad**
    - *Prep:* Chickpeas, cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onion in a lemon-herb dressing.
    - *Storage:* Keeps for 4 days in the fridge.

12. **Salsa Verde Chicken Casserole**
    - *Prep:* Shredded chicken, brown rice, salsa verde, and a sprinkle of cheese.
    - *Cook:* Bake until bubbly.

13. **Curried Chickpea & Spinach Stew**
    - *Prep:* Canned chickpeas simmered in coconut milk and curry powder with fresh spinach added at the end.

14. **Pesto Pasta with Cherry Tomatoes**
    - *Prep:* Whole grain pasta tossed in pesto with halved cherry tomatoes and white beans.

15. **Turkey Burger Bowls**
    - *Prep:* Ground turkey patties (crumbled) served over lettuce with pickles, onions, and a side of roasted potato wedges.

### Storage & Food Safety Tips
*   **Cool Before Closing:** Let food stop steaming before you put the lid on to avoid soggy textures.
*   **The 4-Day Rule:** Most prepped food is best eaten within 4 days. If you prep on Sunday, plan to eat the last meal on Thursday. Freeze Friday's meal if needed.
*   **Label Everything:** Use masking tape to write the date and what's inside.
