<a name="introduction"></a>
### Introduction
Your home is like a living organism. It breathes, it expands, and it contracts. Over time, the seals around your windows, doors, and bathtubs fail. This leads to two major problems: water damage (which is expensive) and energy loss (which is also expensive).

Weatherproofing is the art of sealing those gaps. It’s one of the highest-return DIY tasks you can perform. For the cost of a $10 tube of caulk and an hour of your time, you can prevent a $2,000 bathroom floor repair and save 15% on your heating and cooling bills. Best of all, it requires no heavy machinery or specialized knowledge. This guide will show you how to seal your home like a pro.

### Chapter 2: Caulking the Bath & Shower
If the caulk around your tub is peeling, black (moldy), or missing, water is getting behind your walls.

<a name="remove-caulk"></a>
#### Removing the Old Gunk
You **cannot** just put new caulk over old caulk. It won't stick.
1. Use a utility knife or a specialized caulk scraper to peel away the old material.
2. Clean the area with rubbing alcohol to remove any soap scum or oils.
3. If there's mold, spray it with a bleach solution and let it dry completely before recaulking.

<a name="apply-caulk"></a>
#### Applying a Perfect Bead
1. **Cut the tip:** Cut the caulk tube at a 45-degree angle. The smaller the hole, the more control you have.
2. **The "Steady Hand":** Pull the gun toward you in a steady motion, applying even pressure.
3. **The Smooth:** Dip your finger in a bowl of soapy water and lightly drag it along the bead to smooth it into the corner. Wipe away the excess on a rag.

<a name="windows"></a>
### Chapter 3: Stopping Window Drafts

<a name="draft-test"></a>
#### The Draft Test
On a windy day, light a stick of incense or a candle and hold it near the edges of your windows. If the smoke dances or blows sideways, you've found a leak.

<a name="window-fixes"></a>
#### Quick Fixes
- **Rope Caulk:** A putty-like substance that comes in a roll. You just press it into the gaps. It's great because you can peel it off in the spring.
- **Shrink Film:** A clear plastic sheet that you tape over the window and "shrink" with a hair dryer. It creates an airtight dead-air space that stops cold transfer.

<a name="doors"></a>
### Chapter 4: Weatherstripping Doors
If you can see light under your front door, you're paying to heat the neighborhood.

1. **Install a Door Sweep:** A metal or plastic strip with a rubber fin. Screw it into the bottom of the door so the fin just touches the threshold.
2. **Foam Tape:** Apply adhesive-backed foam tape to the "stop" (the part of the frame the door closes against). When the door closes, it compresses the foam, creating a seal.

<a name="outlets"></a>
### Chapter 5: Sealing Outlets and Switches
Did you know cold air can blow right through your electrical outlets?
1. Buy "Outlet Gaskets"—small foam pads that fit behind the plastic cover plate.
2. Remove the screw, pop the foam on, and screw the plate back. It's a 30-second fix that stops significant drafts.

### Conclusion
By sealing the gaps in your home, you've done more than just save money; you've made your living space more comfortable. No more cold ankles near the window or worrying about water behind the tiles. You've mastered the art of the seal. Well done!
