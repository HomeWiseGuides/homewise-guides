Introduction

Nothing changes the mood of a home faster than lighting. Yet, many people live with flickering bulbs, outdated fixtures, or rooms that are simply too dark because they are intimidated by "electrical work."

Here’s the good news: you don't need to be an electrician to change a light fixture or install a ceiling fan. As long as you follow the golden rule of safety—turning off the power—working with home lighting is straightforward and incredibly rewarding.

Electrical Safety 101

CRITICAL SAFETY RULE: Before you touch a single wire, remember that electricity must be respected. Always turn off the power at the circuit breaker for the room you are working in, not just the wall switch.
  • Turn off the Breaker: Locate your electrical panel and flip the breaker to "Off."
  • Double Check: Use a Non-Contact Voltage Tester to ensure the wires are "dead."
  • Ladder Safety: Use a sturdy ladder on level ground.

Chapter 1: The Ultimate Bulb Guide

Bulb Types

LED Lasts 20+ years. Most efficient.
CFL Contains mercury. Slow start. Avoid.
Halogen Gets very hot. High energy use.

Lumens vs. Watts

Stop looking at Watts! Lumens measure brightness. For a standard room, aim for 800 Lumens (60W equivalent). For a bright workspace, aim for 1600 Lumens (100W equivalent).

Chapter 2: Installing a New Light Fixture

Replacing a "boob light" or an old pendant is a 30-minute job.

  1. 1 Remove old fixture: Unscrew the base and disconnect the wire nuts.
  2. 2 Connect Wires: Match Black to Black (Hot), White to White (Neutral), and Green to Green/Bare (Ground).
  3. 3 Mount: Tuck wires into the ceiling box and screw the new fixture to the mounting bracket.

Chapter 3: Ceiling Fan Maintenance

Fixing a Wobbly Fan

  1. Clean: Dust buildup on blades is the #1 cause of wobbling.
  2. Tighten: Use a screwdriver to tighten the screws connecting the blades to the motor.
  3. Balance: Use a balancing kit (weights) if the wobble persists after cleaning.
