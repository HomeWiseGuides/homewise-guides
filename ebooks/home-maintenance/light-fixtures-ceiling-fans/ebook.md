<a name="introduction"></a>
### Introduction
Nothing changes the mood of a home faster than lighting. Yet, many people live with flickering bulbs, outdated fixtures, or rooms that are simply too dark because they are intimidated by "electrical work."

Here’s the good news: you don't need to be an electrician to change a light fixture or install a ceiling fan. As long as you follow the golden rule of safety—turning off the power—working with home lighting is straightforward and incredibly rewarding. This guide will take you from choosing the right LED bulb to hanging a beautiful new chandelier or a cooling ceiling fan, all on your own.

<a name="safety"></a>
### Electrical Safety 101
Before you touch a single wire, remember: **Electricity must be respected.**
1. **Turn off the switch:** For simple bulb changes, just turn off the wall switch.
2. **Turn off the Breaker:** For fixtures and fans, you **must** go to your electrical panel and flip the breaker to "Off" for that specific room.
3. **Double Check:** Use a "Non-Contact Voltage Tester" (a $15 tool that beeps near live wires) to ensure the power is truly off before you start.
4. **Ladder Safety:** Always use a sturdy ladder. Never stand on the very top step, and have a friend hold the base if you're going high.

### Chapter 1: The Ultimate Bulb Guide

<a name="bulb-types"></a>
#### Types of Bulbs
- **LED:** The gold standard. Lasts 20+ years, uses very little energy, and stays cool to the touch.
- **CFL:** Spiral bulbs. Cheap but contain mercury and take time to warm up. Outdated.
- **Incandescent/Halogen:** Get very hot and burn out quickly. Replace these with LEDs immediately to save money.

<a name="lumens"></a>
#### Lumens vs. Watts
In the old days, we bought bulbs by "Watts" (how much power they used). Today, look at **Lumens** (how much light they give).
- **450 Lumens:** Dim (equivalent to 40W)
- **800 Lumens:** Standard (equivalent to 60W)
- **1600 Lumens:** Very Bright (equivalent to 100W)

### Chapter 2: Changing Any Bulb Safely

<a name="recessed"></a>
#### Recessed (Pot) Lights
These are flush with the ceiling.
1. Let the bulb cool completely.
2. Push up slightly and turn counter-clockwise. 
3. If it’s stuck, use a piece of duct tape: fold it into a loop, stick it to the bulb face, and use the loop as a handle to twist.

<a name="tube"></a>
#### Tube Lights (Fluorescent)
1. Rotate the tube 90 degrees until the pins align with the slots in the fixture.
2. Slide the tube out.
3. **Tip:** Replace these with "LED Tube Replacements" for better light and no humming noise.

### Chapter 3: Installing a New Light Fixture
Ready to swap that ugly "boob light" for a modern pendant?

1. **Remove the old fixture:** Unscrew the canopy and disconnect the wire nuts (plastic caps).
2. **Identify the wires:** You will usually see three:
   - **Black:** Hot (Power)
   - **White:** Neutral
   - **Green or Bare Copper:** Ground (Safety)
3. **Install the bracket:** Screw the new mounting bracket to the junction box in the ceiling.
4. **Connect wires:** Match the colors. Twist the bare ends together and screw on a wire nut.
5. **Mount:** Tuck the wires into the box and screw the fixture canopy to the bracket.

<a name="fans"></a>
### Chapter 4: Ceiling Fan Installation & Maintenance

<a name="fan-install"></a>
#### Installation Guide
Ceiling fans are heavier than light fixtures. You **must** ensure the junction box in the ceiling is "Fan Rated" (bolted to a joist).
1. **Assemble the fan:** Put the blades on the motor while it's still on the ground.
2. **Hang the motor:** Most fans have a hook that lets you hang the motor while you connect the wires.
3. **Wire it up:** Same as a light fixture—Black to Black, White to White, Green to Green.

<a name="fan-wobble"></a>
#### Fixing a Wobbly Fan
A wobbly fan isn't just annoying; it's inefficient.
1. **Clean the blades:** Dust buildup can unbalance a fan.
2. **Tighten everything:** Check the screws holding the blades to the motor.
3. **Use a Balancing Kit:** If it still wobbles, use the small clip and weights that come with most fans to find the "heavy" blade and offset it.
