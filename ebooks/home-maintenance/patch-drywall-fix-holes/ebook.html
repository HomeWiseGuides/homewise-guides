The Repair Kit

  • Lightweight Spackle
  • Joint Compound
  • Putty Knives (2" & 6")
  • Mesh Tape
  • Aluminum Wall Patches
  • Sanding Sponge
LEVEL 1

Tiny Nail Holes

Perfect for fixing walls before moving out of a rental or rearranging art.

  1. Push any raised edges into the hole with your putty knife handle.
  2. Apply a dab of spackle and swipe flush.
  3. Let dry for 30 minutes and sand lightly.
LEVEL 2

Doorknob Dents & Medium Holes

These require structural support so the spackle doesn't fall into the wall.

  1. Stick an aluminum-backed mesh patch over the hole.
  2. Apply joint compound over the patch.
  3. Feather the edges: Thin out the compound 3 inches beyond the patch for a smooth transition.
Pro Tip: Two thin coats are much easier to sand and look better than one thick, lumpy coat.

Sanding & Painting

Drywall compound is porous. If you paint directly over it, it will look different from the rest of the wall.

  • Sanding: Run your hand over the repair. It should feel perfectly smooth.
  • Priming: Use a dab of primer first.
  • Texturing: Dab the paint on with a brush to recreate the "stipple" of the surrounding wall.
