### Introduction
The toilet is the most essential seat in the house, yet it's the one we fear fixing the most. Most people see a puddle on the floor or hear a phantom "flush" in the middle of the night and immediately reach for the phone to call a plumber. 

Here is the truth: A toilet is a very simple machine. It uses gravity and a few rubber seals to do its job. Almost everything that can go wrong with a toilet can be fixed with a $15 part and 20 minutes of your time. This guide will walk you through everything from stopping that annoying "running" sound to completely replacing the unit and even upgrading to a modern bidet.

### Chapter 1: The "Running" Toilet Fix
If your toilet runs constantly or turns on randomly for a few seconds, you are wasting hundreds of gallons of water a month.

<a name="flapper"></a>
#### Replacing the Flapper
The flapper is the rubber "stopper" at the bottom of the tank. Over time, it warps and lets water leak into the bowl.
1. **Turn off the water:** Close the valve behind the toilet.
2. **Flush:** Hold the handle down to empty the tank.
3. **Unhook:** Remove the chain from the handle arm and pop the flapper off the pegs on the overflow tube.
4. **Install:** Snap the new flapper onto the pegs and connect the chain.
5. **Test:** Turn the water back on. If it still leaks, adjust the chain length—it should have a tiny bit of slack.

<a name="fill-valve"></a>
#### Adjusting the Fill Valve
If the water is running into the overflow tube (the open pipe in the center), your water level is too high.
1. Locate the adjustment screw on the top of the fill valve (the tall tower on the left).
2. Turn it to lower the float ball or cup. The water should stop about 1 inch below the top of the overflow tube.

### Chapter 2: Clearing Stubborn Clogs

<a name="plunge"></a>
#### The Right Way to Plunge
Don't use a flat "sink" plunger. You need a **Flange Plunger** (one with a fold-out rubber neck).
1. Ensure there is enough water in the bowl to cover the plunger head.
2. Insert the plunger at an angle to avoid trapping air.
3. Push down and pull up with short, fast strokes. The *pulling* action is often what breaks the clog.

<a name="auger"></a>
#### Using a Closet Auger
If the plunger fails, you need a closet auger. This is a cable inside a metal tube that protects your toilet's porcelain from scratches.
1. Pull the cable all the way up into the tube.
2. Place the curved end into the drain hole.
3. Crank the handle while pushing down. Once the cable is all the way in, pull it back out while cranking. Flush to see if it's clear.

<a name="bidet"></a>
### Chapter 3: Installing a Bidet Seat/Attachment
Bidets are cleaner, more eco-friendly, and easier to install than you think.

1. **Remove the Seat:** Unscrew the two bolts behind the seat.
2. **Install the T-Valve:** Turn off the water and unscrew the supply line from the *bottom of the tank*. Screw on the metal T-valve that came with your bidet. Reattach the supply line to the bottom of the T-valve.
3. **Attach Bidet:** Place the bidet attachment on the toilet rim and put the seat back on over it.
4. **Connect Hose:** Connect the small hose from the T-valve to the bidet.
5. **Turn on and Test:** Check for leaks at every connection.

<a name="replacement"></a>
### Chapter 4: Full Toilet Replacement

<a name="removal"></a>
#### Removing the Old Toilet
1. **Drain completely:** Turn off the water, flush, and use a sponge to soak up the remaining water in both the tank and the bowl.
2. **Disconnect:** Unscrew the water supply line. Remove the plastic caps at the base of the toilet and unscrew the floor bolts.
3. **Lift:** Rock the toilet gently to break the wax seal, then lift it straight up. **Warning:** Toilets are heavy—get help if needed!

<a name="flange"></a>
#### Preparing the Flange
1. Scrape away the old, sticky wax from the floor flange using a putty knife.
2. Check the flange (the plastic or metal ring in the floor). If it's cracked, buy a "flange repair ring."
3. Insert new closet bolts into the flange slots.

<a name="new-toilet"></a>
#### Installing the New Toilet
1. **Apply Wax:** Press the new wax ring onto the bottom of the new toilet's discharge hole.
2. **Set the Toilet:** Lower the toilet over the floor bolts.
3. **Press down:** Sit on the toilet to compress the wax ring and create a seal.
4. **Tighten:** Put washers and nuts on the bolts. Tighten until snug, but **DO NOT OVER-TIGHTEN** or you will crack the porcelain.
5. **Hook up water:** Reconnect the supply line and turn on the valve.
