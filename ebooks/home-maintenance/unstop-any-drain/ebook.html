Why Avoid Chemical Cleaners?

Liquid drain cleaners are corrosive. They generate heat that can damage PVC and eat through older metal pipes. If they fail to clear the clog, you're left with a sink full of toxic acid.

Safety First: Never plunge a drain after pouring chemicals into it. The splash-back can cause severe chemical burns.

Mastering the Plunge

For Sinks:

  1. Seal the Overflow: Cover the small overflow hole with a wet rag.
  2. Create a Seal: Ensure water covers the lip of the plunger.
  3. Push & Pull: Focus on the pull—the vacuum is what breaks the clog.

The P-Trap Solution

If plunging fails, the clog is in the U-shaped pipe under the sink.

  1. Place a bucket under the trap.
  2. Unscrew the large plastic nuts by hand.
  3. Remove the pipe and wash it out in another sink.

Prevention Tips

  • Mesh Strainers: Use them in every sink and shower.
  • The 3 Ps: Only Paper, Pee, and Poop go in the toilet.
  • Weekly Flush: Pour a gallon of boiling water down drains once a week to melt soap scum.
